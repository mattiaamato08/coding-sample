# coding-sample
The initial corpus consists of all speeches delivered in the European Parliament plenary sessions between 1999 and 2026. Before any textual analysis, the raw dataset undergoes a harmonization stage aimed at improving both metadata consistency and text quality. In particular, party labels are standardized across legislative terms and missing PartyFacts identifiers are recovered whenever a stable correspondence with European political groups exists. In addition, speeches associated with failed or low-quality machine translations are identified through manually audited files and removed from the corpus to avoid introducing translation noise into the subsequent analyses. The textual preprocessing stage is performed using the English version of each speech whenever a reliable translation is available; otherwise, the original English text is retained. Each document is converted to lowercase, URLs, numerical characters and punctuation are removed, and the text is lemmatized using the spaCy natural language processing library. Standard English stopwords are discarded, together with single-character tokens and non-alphabetic elements, producing a normalized representation that reduces lexical variability while preserving semantic content.
After preprocessing, several corpus diagnostics are computed, including speech length distributions, word frequencies and document frequencies. These statistics are used to inspect the overall structure of the corpus and to identify potential sources of noise. Speeches containing five or fewer tokens are excluded, as they typically correspond to procedural remarks or non-informative interventions. Furthermore, interventions delivered by the President or the Chair are removed through a multilingual rule-based identification procedure based on speaker names, ensuring that the analysis focuses exclusively on substantive parliamentary contributions. Finally, a small set of corpus-specific procedural expressions (e.g., rapporteur, commissioner, madam) is eliminated following manual inspection of the most frequent terms.
To improve the semantic representation of the corpus, multi-word expressions are incorporated through a data-driven bigram extraction procedure. Candidate bigrams are identified under different minimum document frequency thresholds and evaluated using Pointwise Mutual Information (PMI) and its frequency-weighted variant, Local PMI (LMI). While standard PMI tends to overemphasize extremely rare word pairs, Local PMI balances association strength with empirical frequency, yielding more robust and interpretable collocations. The final selection of bigrams is based on the inspection of the Local PMI rank curve together with cumulative coverage statistics, allowing the identification of a cutoff that captures the most informative lexical combinations while excluding the long tail of weak associations. The selected bigrams are then merged into single lexical units (e.g., member_state, single_market, fiscal_rule), generating the final corpus used for all subsequent embedding and topic modeling analyses.
